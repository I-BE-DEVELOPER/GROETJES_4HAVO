Grammy awards 2020 best outfits



Ariana Grande in: Giambattista Valli

There had to be one big dress, right? There always is. But Grande’s is interesting because it’s a rather somber shade of gray. The effect is arresting and also a little melancholy. She posed sitting into her dress, letting the cloud poof up around her while she pressed her gloves to her face.

Billy Porter in: Sarah Sokol Millinery

Yes, yes, another Billy Porter mention on the best-dressed page. This time, instead of riding in on a litter carried by bare-chested men or wearing the curtain from his hit play, he channeled musicians like Sia and Orville Peck with a face-obscuring hat. When it was time to reveal his face to the camera, the fringe parted automatically. What a performance.

Billie Eilish in: Gucci

Billie Eilish has a set look. We expect her to wear something oversize in neon green and black. What’s impressive is how many permutations of this outfit she manages to find. This Gucci ensemble is especially nice because of the fingertip-less gloves which show off her manicure, and the bejeweled face mask.

Nikita Dragun in: Augusto Manzanares

Nikita Dragun bared it all as she walked the Grammys red carpet, risking a wardrobe malfunction with every step in her nude illusion dress. The beauty guru wore a gorgeous pearl dress to the award show, complete with some Euphoria-esque pearly makeup, a brown feather boa and the highest heels I've ever seen. The dress is almost completely see-through, showing off Nikita's side-boob and side-booty (yeah, that's a thing now). Nikita worked all angles of the dress on the red carpet, along with her newly blonde hair. In fact, the YouTuber was so nervous about a slip-up that she told Entertainment Tonight that she brought superglue with her to the awards. "Just in case," she said. "For any body parts that fall off, or a nail." Is there an award tonight for bravest attendee?

Demi lovato in: Christian Siriano

Among the many performances at the Grammys 2020 tonight, perhaps one of the most anticipated was that of Demi Lovato. After a year and a half since her overdose, Demi returned to the stage singing a new song that brought the audience to their tears and to their feet for a standing ovation. As Demi shed tears while belting her heart out, she stood onstage in a breathtaking white gown, completed with a studded corset belt. Pairing it with some diamond earrings, the entire look was simple yet angelic, clean and the perfect way to highlight Demi's return to the stage. Unsurprisingly, it was designed by Christian Siriano.










































Alicia Keys in: Versace

She was the hostess with the mostess at the 2020 Grammy Awards, and although she had approx. 600 outfit changes (including one v casual jeans and a t-shirt look towards the end of the night which, tbh, we vibe) it was Alicia Keys' first dress that totally stole the show. The look was a heritage Atelier Versace asymmetric number with a thigh-high split and strong shoulder. It also featured heavy crystal embroidery at the neckline which fell into the loose draping as it cascaded into the dress's metal mesh form. Yup, you read that right. While it may look like a sequined extravaganza at first glance, the reflective silver material is in fact a tightly woven metal mesh fabric. A phrase we never thought we'd swoon at, until now.
